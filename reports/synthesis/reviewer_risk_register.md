# Reviewer Risk Register

This register anticipates the most likely reviewer objections for the benchmark
paper and maps each risk to current mitigation and remaining limitations.

| Risk | Current mitigation | Source artifact | Remaining limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reviewers may ask why random splits are not reported as headline evidence. | The validation protocol requires condition/stressor grouped splits and treats random row/cell splits as leakage smoke tests only. | `docs/VALIDATION_PROTOCOL.md` | Need a concise methods paragraph explaining that 228 cells are 76 condition triplets, not independent regimes. |
| C-rate evidence has small condition count. | Reports aggregate by parameter set and explicitly list C-rate rows/conditions; claim ledger keeps C-rate fade claims blocked. | `docs/experiments/2026-05-23_prior_pulse_vs_best_nonpulse.md` | C-rate split still has only 12 held-out parameter-set conditions in several analyses. |
| PULSE alignment deltas may be large. | Alignment sensitivity was run at 6h/12h/24h/36h/all thresholds; 24h retains all C-rate rows and all 76 parameter sets. | `docs/experiments/2026-05-23_pulse_alignment_direction_hardening.md` | Alignment is still a reporting sensitivity, not proof of a perfect temporal match. |
| Direction averaging may hide charge/discharge differences. | Direction-specific extraction showed current RT/50 mean is effectively charge; discharge adjacent deltas are unavailable and direction-specific claims remain blocked. | `reports/baselines/pulse_resistance_l0_l3/pulse_direction_policy_summary.md` | No discharge RT/50 adjacent-delta claim can be made from current extraction. |
| LOG_AGE features may leak future diagnostics. | Inserted LOG_AGE diagnostics are masked; target-derived stress rates are excluded from predictive groups; future PULSE states and deltas are forbidden. | `docs/VALIDATION_PROTOCOL.md`, `docs/PAPER_CLAIM_LEDGER.md` | Manuscript must clearly distinguish diagnostic columns from predictive features. |
| Prior PULSE does not beat strongest non-PULSE baselines. | Milestone 0.9.1 explicitly tests and reports this negative result. | `reports/baselines/capacity_prior_pulse_vs_best_nonpulse/prior_pulse_vs_best_nonpulse_claim_readiness.md` | Headline wording must stay at "prior PULSE improves over F4 in selected splits," not "best model." |
| Prior PULSE does not improve `delta_capacity_Ah`. | Claim ledger marks fade-rate improvement as not supported. | `docs/PAPER_CLAIM_LEDGER.md` | Capacity-level and fade-rate results must be separated in abstract, figures, and discussion. |
| Quantile HGB intervals are not calibrated. | Claim ledger and negative-result report mark quantile calibration as not supported. | `reports/baselines/capacity_hgb50_focused/claim_readiness.md` | No uncertainty claim should appear unless a future calibration milestone is run. |
| EIS is not tested despite being in the project title/charter. | EIS is explicitly gated; the paper can frame EIS as future gated work rather than current evidence. | `docs/REPO_STATUS.md`, `docs/PROJECT_CHARTER.md` | Title and abstract should avoid implying EIS performance results. |
| No same-cell counterfactuals exist. | Charter and validation protocol frame policy/generalization claims as support-bounded grouped comparisons, not same-cell counterfactuals. | `docs/PROJECT_CHARTER.md`, `docs/PAPER_CLAIM_LEDGER.md` | Avoid causal/policy-response claims in current manuscript. |
| LOG_AGE stress features did not solve C-rate fade prediction. | Negative results report treats this as evidence and redirects the story to benchmark boundaries. | `reports/synthesis/negative_results.md` | The paper must present this as a finding, not a failure to hide. |
| Best-row tables could be misread as claim support. | Split difficulty summary and synthesis memo now state that best-known rows are descriptive and do not override paired claim-readiness tests. | `reports/synthesis/split_difficulty_summary.csv`, `docs/experiments/2026-05-23_evidence_synthesis.md` | Figure captions should repeat this caveat when showing best-row summaries. |
